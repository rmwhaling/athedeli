All About Ham

Black Forest Ham

Black Forest ham, also known as Schwarzwälder Schinken in German, is a type of dry-cured ham that originates from the Black Forest region in Germany. It is a highly esteemed delicacy known for its distinct flavor and traditional production methods. Key characteristics of Black Forest ham include: Curing Process: Black Forest ham is traditionally dry-cured with a mixture of salt, garlic, and various herbs and spices. The curing process helps preserve the ham and imparts flavor. Smoking: After the initial curing process, Black Forest ham is smoked over pine wood, which is indigenous to the Black Forest region. The smoking process gives the ham its characteristic aroma and enhances its flavor. Aging: Once cured and smoked, the ham is aged for several months to develop its flavor and texture. The aging process helps the ham develop its complex, slightly sweet taste. Appearance: Black Forest ham typically has a dark, reddish-brown exterior with a distinctive marbling of fat throughout the meat. Black Forest ham is commonly sliced thinly and served as a cold cut in sandwiches, salads, or as part of charcuterie boards. It is prized for its rich, savory flavor with subtle smoky and herbal notes. The name "Black Forest" comes from the dense, evergreen forests of the region where the ham is traditionally produced.


What is Black Forest Ham The original Black Forest ham is a variety of dry-cured smoked ham. It typically takes up to three months to produce from start to finish. The first step in producing this deli favorite is to salt and season the raw ham with pepper, garlic, juniper berries, coriander and other spices. The meat is given two weeks to cure, after which the salt is removed. Once the salt is taken out, the meat is given an additional two weeks to age. After that four-week stretch of curing, the meat is cold-smoked using juniper brush or sawdust and fir. The temperature of the container holding the ham must not reach more than 77 degrees Fahrenheit for several weeks. It is during this period which the ham develops its trademark black skin. Though the ham may look bad, the black skin is actually what gives it its distinctive flavor. Once removed from cold storage, the ham is air-cured for an additional two weeks before going on the market.


It's namesake ham is known for it's delicately blackened skin and light, smoky flavor. That dark edge comes from spices applied prior to smoking. Salt, garlic, coriander, juniper and pepper to name a few. Black Forest ham is smoked over pine or fir and the whole process can take up to three months.

Virginia Ham

Virginia ham refers to a type of cured ham that is produced primarily in the state of Virginia, USA. It is renowned for its distinctive flavor and texture, which are the result of a specific curing process. The process typically involves dry curing the ham with a mixture of salt, sugar, and sometimes other seasonings. After the curing process, the ham is often smoked over hardwoods such as hickory or oak, which further enhances its flavor. The hams are then aged for an extended period, sometimes up to a year or more, to develop their characteristic taste and texture. Virginia ham is often served thinly sliced and can be enjoyed on its own or as part of various dishes, such as sandwiches, salads, or as a complement to other foods. It is considered a delicacy and is prized for its rich, savory flavor. There are different varieties of Virginia ham, including country ham, which is typically aged longer and has a more intense flavor, and Virginia's famous Smithfield ham, which is produced in the town of Smithfield, Virginia, and is known for its quality and taste.

Cooked Ham

Cooked ham is a type of ham that has been prepared by cooking or curing. It is usually made from the hind leg of a pig, which is trimmed, brined, and cooked to achieve a tender texture and savory flavor. The cooking process typically involves boiling or baking the ham until it reaches a safe internal temperature. Sometimes, it may also be smoked for additional flavor. Cooked ham is commonly sliced and served cold, often in sandwiches, salads, or as a main dish. It's important to note that there are various types of ham, including cured ham, smoked ham, and deli ham, each with its own distinct flavor and preparation method.

Tavern Ham

Tavern ham is a type of ham that is typically served in taverns, restaurants, and delis. It is similar to traditional ham but often has a slightly different flavor profile and texture. Tavern ham is usually made from pork that has been cured and then smoked or cooked. The curing process involves soaking the pork in a brine solution or dry curing it with salt and other seasonings. After curing, the ham may be smoked to add flavor or cooked by baking or boiling until it reaches a safe internal temperature. The flavor of tavern ham can vary depending on the specific curing and smoking techniques used by different producers. It is often sliced thinly and served cold in sandwiches, salads, or as part of a charcuterie board. Overall, tavern ham is a popular choice for its savory taste and versatility in various culinary applications.
