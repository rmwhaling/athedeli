Hard vs. Genoa Salami

Hard salami is tangy, peppery, and rich. Genoa salami tends to be sweeter, garlicky, and milder in taste. Genoa salami has a distinct sweet flavor with garlic and wine notes.

Uses

  • Sandwiches
  • Charcuterie Boards
  • Salads
  • Pizza Toppings

Ingredients

Hard Salami: Hard salami usually consists of a mixture of pork and beef, although sometimes it might be made entirely from one of these meats. It's seasoned with various spices, including garlic, pepper, and sometimes paprika.
Genoa Salami: Genoa salami is traditionally made with pork, though it may contain beef as well. It often includes garlic, salt, white or red wine, and sometimes black peppercorns or other spices. Genoa salami is also known for its coarse grind, which gives it a distinct texture.

Origin

Hard Salami: Hard salami is a broad term for a style of dry, fermented sausage that has been made for centuries in various regions around the world, including Italy, Germany, and the United States.
Genoa Salami: Genoa salami originates from the city of Genoa in Italy and is a specific type of Italian salami.
